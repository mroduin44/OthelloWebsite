Desdemona: Heaven

"My noble father, I do perceive here a divided duty. To you I am bound for life and education. My life and education both do learn me How to respect you. You are the lord of duty. I am hitherto your daughter. But here's my husband, And so much duty as my mother showed To you, preferring you before her father, So much I challenge that I may profess Due to the Moor my lord" (1.3.208-218)

"That I did love the Moor to live with him My downright violence and storm of fortunes May trumpet to the world. My heart's subdued Even to the very quality of my lord. I saw Othello's visage in his mind, And to his honor and his valiant parts Did I my soul and fortunes consecrate" (1.3.283-294)

Desdemona is madly in love with Othello to the point where nothing is more important to her than him not even her own life. The song connects to this with the idea that nothing could be better than being with him. Desdemona continuously shows her complete devotion to Othello from leaving her father all the way to her death willing to give everything to him.

Act 1 Scene 1: What was I thinking?

"I am one, sir, that comes to tell you your daughter and the Moor are now making the beast with two backs" (1.1.119; 123-127; 129-131)

"Though I do hate him as I do hell pains, Yet, for necessity of present life, I must show out a flag and sign of love— Which is indeed but sign" (1.1.171-174)

This song connects to Act 1 Scene 1 through Othello as a flashback highlighting the change in his idea of Desdemona throughout the play starting with being madly in love and developing into a strong hatred. Othello makes what he eventually believes to be a huge mistake and regrets his decision big time.
